SimBenchmark

SimBenchmark is a project for benchmark of rigid body simulators with contacts.

Introduction

An Accurate and fast simulation of contact dynamics is crucial for research on legged robots since the motion of legged robots is highly dependent on contact force generated by feet-terrain interaction. So far, a number of rigid body simulators with contact solvers has been introduced to the robotics society. However most state-of-the-art simulators face trade-off between reality of the simulation and simulation efficient.

In this benchmark, we provide evaluation of the most commonly-used rigid body simulators in various situations from single-bodies with a limited number of contacts to complex articulated robotic systems with PD control input. We hope this benchmark results would help robotics researchers to find and select the best one for their purposes.

Methodology

We measure accuracy and CPU time of simulation with different time step. Since accuracy of numerical solution is usually better when the time step is smaller, we considered this speed-accuracy trade off as proposed in Tom Erez et al. In the following example of speed-accuracy curve, the engine 1 represented by curve 1 is better than the engine 2 represented by curve 2.

For accuracy metric, we use squared velocity error of simulation when an analytical solution of trajectory is applicable. Besides, for complex systems that analytical solution of trajectory is not available, we investigate penetration error, momentum or energy.
